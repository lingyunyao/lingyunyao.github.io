About Me

I am a PhD researcher at Aalto University (Finland), advised by Professor Martin Andraud. I received my M.Sc. in Micro- and Nanoelectronic Circuit Design from Aalto in 2022, where I explored number systems for probabilistic circuits and their application to self-adaptive RF transceivers. My research builds energy-efficient hardware and algorithms for probabilistic and neuro-symbolic AI — from GPU kernels for log-domain inference to a taped-out 16 nm accelerator — with the goal of making uncertainty-aware and explainable AI efficient enough to run everywhere.

I have been a visiting scholar at MICAS, KU Leuven (Belgium) under Professor Marian Verhelst, where I led the backend and silicon testing of our neuro-symbolic AI chip.

Research Highlights

GPU acceleration of log-domain inference

Approximate LogSumExp CUDA kernels for general-purpose GPUs, achieving 6–10× speedups over standard PyTorch kernels for probabilistic inference. (Submitted to NeurIPS 2026)

Neuro-symbolic AI accelerator — 16 nm ASIC tape-out

Designed and silicon-tested a 4 mm² 16 nm chip for both neural and symbolic acceleration, reaching 8.5× higher throughput than the symbolic baseline with near-state-of-the-art neural performance. (IEEE CICC 2026)

Energy-efficient inference via approximate computing

FPGA and ASIC inference engines using approximate log-domain arithmetic with software-side error correction, delivering up to 649× energy reduction and 46% area / 32% power versus floating-point baselines, with negligible numerical error.

Hardware/software co-design toolflow & benchmarking

Co-developed an open-source toolchain that maps trained probabilistic models to FPGA, and ran systematic FPGA/GPU benchmarking to derive design guidelines for future probabilistic AI accelerators.
